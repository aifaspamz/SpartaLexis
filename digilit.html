What is Digital Literacy?

Digital literacy is the ability to use technology safely, responsibly, and effectively. It’s not just about using devices — it’s about navigating the digital world with confidence, communicating clearly, and making informed decisions.

How to Become Digitally Literate:

  • Use Digital Tools – Learn apps, software, and devices. Tip: Practice shortcuts and watch tutorials.
  • Evaluate Information – Verify sources before trusting content. Tip: Cross-check multiple sources.
  • Communicate Clearly – Share ideas responsibly. Tip: Check tone before sending messages.
  • Stay Safe Online – Protect your data. Tip: Use strong passwords & enable MFA.

Why is Digital Literacy Important?

Digital literacy empowers people to use technology effectively and safely. It helps users identify trustworthy information, avoid online scams, and think critically before sharing anything.

Tip: Always question suspicious content and verify before sharing.

Problem Solving with Technology

Use digital tools to troubleshoot issues, explore guides, and simplify tasks using the right apps and software.

Tip: Update devices regularly and avoid suspicious websites.

Responsible Use

Engage ethically online—protect personal data, respect others, and collaborate professionally.

Tip: Always credit collaborators and respect privacy settings.

Critical Thinking

Analyze information before sharing. Check sources, compare facts, and reflect before reacting online.

Tip: Verify sources and avoid spreading misinformation.
